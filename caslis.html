INTRODUCTION

What is Calisthenics?

Calisthenics is a form of exercise that uses bodyweight movements to build strength, flexibility, and endurance. The word comes from the Greek words "kallos" (beauty) and "sthenos" (strength).

This training method requires minimal equipment and can be performed anywhere. It focuses on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups simultaneously.

Calisthenics has been practiced for centuries and remains one of the most effective ways to develop functional fitness.

EXERCISES

Basic Movements

Push-Ups

Targets chest, shoulders, and triceps. A fundamental pushing exercise.

  • Standard push-up
  • Wide grip
  • Diamond push-up
  • Archer push-up

Pull-Ups

Targets back, biceps, and forearms. Essential for upper body development.

  • Overhand grip
  • Underhand grip
  • Wide grip
  • Close grip

Squats

Targets quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Builds lower body strength.

  • Bodyweight squat
  • Jump squat
  • Pistol squat
  • Bulgarian split squat

Dips

Targets triceps, chest, and shoulders. Performed on parallel bars.

  • Bench dips
  • Parallel bar dips
  • Ring dips
  • Straight bar dips

Core Training

Targets abdominals and lower back. Essential for stability and control.

  • Plank
  • Leg raises
  • L-sit
  • Hollow body hold

Lunges

Targets legs and improves balance. Great for unilateral strength.

  • Forward lunge
  • Reverse lunge
  • Walking lunge
  • Jump lunge

TRAINING

Progression Levels

BEGINNER

Foundation

Focus on learning proper form and building basic strength.

  • Wall push-ups
  • Assisted squats
  • Dead hangs
  • Knee planks
INTERMEDIATE

Strength Building

Increase volume and learn more challenging variations.

  • Standard push-ups
  • Pull-ups
  • Pistol squat progressions
  • L-sit progressions
ADVANCED

Skill Development

Master complex movements requiring high strength and control.

  • Muscle-ups
  • Handstand push-ups
  • Front lever
  • Planche progressions
